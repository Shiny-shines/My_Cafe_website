Coffee Mocha

Caffe Mocha is simply a chocolate-flavored caffe latte. It’s made with chocolate, coffee, and milk. It’s also called mochaccino or mocha latte. Its name comes from the city of Mocha (or al-Makha) in Yemen which was the major marketplace for coffee from the 15th century until the 18th century.

Americano

An Americano is an espresso drink made with hot water and espresso, also called a Caffe Americano. The drink can be made with either one or two shots of espresso, and varying ratios of water. Diluting the strong dark espresso roast brings the flavor closer to a drip coffee: which might be where the name originated!

Frappucino & Frappe

Frappe originated in Greece and is made with instant coffee, sugar, water, and milk blended together with ice, while Frappuccino is a trademarked iced coffee beverage sold by Starbucks which consists of espresso shots, milk, and sweeteners blended with ice. Frappuccinos tend to be sweeter than frappes due to the use of flavored syrups and whipped cream toppings, while frappes tend to be sweeter than Frappuccinos, but have a stronger coffee flavor.

Iced latte

An iced latte blends chilled milk with a robust shot of espresso and ice, creating a creamy, refreshing experience with every sip.It is usually served in a tall glass, often with a straw. It has a strong coffee flavor and a smooth, creamy texture. It can sometimes be topped with whipped cream or flavored syrups for an extra indulgent treat.

Mazagran

Mazagran (also called café mazagran, formerly spelled masagran) is a cold, sweetened coffee drink that originated in Algeria.Portuguese versions may use espresso, lemon, mint and rum, and Austrian versions are served with an ice cube and include rum. Sometimes a fast version is achieved by pouring a previously sweetened espresso in a cup with ice cubes and a slice of lemon. Mazagran has been described as "the original iced coffee".
