Iced Latte

The key in how to make an iced latte is finding the perfect coffee-to-milk ratio. Here’s a guide to help you navigate this flavorful adventure:
Standard Ratio: The classic iced latte typically follows a 1:2 ratio of coffee to milk. This means:
1 part coffee (usually 1-2 shots of espresso or ½ cup of strong brewed coffee) 2 parts milk (about 1 cup)
Adjusting for Taste Everyone’s palate is different, so feel free to tweak the ratio to suit your preferences: Stronger Coffee Flavor: Try a 1:1 ratio. This is perfect for those who love a bold coffee taste.
Creamier Texture: Opt for a 1:3 ratio. This will give you a richer, milkier drink.
Experiment with Different Coffees Different types of coffee can affect the ideal ratio:
Espresso: Typically stronger, so you might stick closer to the standard 1:2 ratio. Cold Brew: Naturally smoother and less acidic, allowing a 1:1.5 ratio for a balanced flavor.
